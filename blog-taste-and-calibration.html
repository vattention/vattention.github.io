最近，我们任命 Bobby Chen 为 Vattention 的 Partner。

这不只是一个新 title。它是一种正式承认：某件事其实已经发生了。Bobby 已经在以 Partner 的责任、判断力和 ownership 工作。

短短几个月里，他接下了关键问题，帮助团队把模糊变成结构，并把执行标准推向更高处。在传统组织里，这样的节奏也许显得很快。但在一家 AI 创业公司里，我们认为这正是应该有的速度。

Partner 是一种动态责任

在 Vattention，Partner 是一种动态机制，而不是一个固定级别。

它不是一个拿到之后就可以长期收藏的永久徽章。当然，我们希望每一位 Partner 都能长期带领公司。但在结构上，我们把 Partner 理解为一种解耦 authority、responsibility 和 economics 的方式。

责任来自一个人实际承担的使命。
权力来自他在某个阶段对方向塑造和关键决策的参与。
经济回报应该反映长期贡献和真实影响。

因此，Partner 不是由年限授予的，而是由责任、贡献，以及推动真实变化的能力识别出来的。

Bobby 进入这个角色，并不是系统之外的例外。恰恰相反，这是系统如预期运转的结果。

从线性人力到 100-1000 倍杠杆

在很多组织里，增加人手通常被默认等于增加产出。

多一个人意味着多一份 capacity。一个强的人也许能贡献 1.5 倍，一个普通的人也许仍然能贡献 1.0 倍。

但在 AI 时代，这个假设越来越不可靠。

每增加一个人，都会增加沟通成本、对齐成本、协作成本和组织熵。他是否能增加产出是不确定的；但他几乎一定会增加系统复杂度。

我们正在从一个 1.5 vs. 1.0 的世界，进入一个 100-1000 vs. -3 的世界。

真正合适的人可以创造 100-1000 倍杠杆。他们能重新定义问题，重新设计流程，把 AI 放到系统里正确的位置，并把整个团队的能力边界向前推。

错误的人并不是一个 0.8 倍贡献者。他可能是负杠杆：增加复杂度、拖慢决策，让组织变得更擅长解释自己，而不是面对现实。

这就是为什么我们用两个词描述自己的人才哲学：

Taste + Calibration.

Taste：知道如何领导 AI

我们说 Taste，并不是通常意义上的偏好或审美。

我们指的是架构品味：一种理解如何领导 AI 的判断力。

使用 AI 和领导 AI 是两种不同的能力。

使用 AI，是把它当成一个更快的工具。
领导 AI，是围绕它重新设计系统：AI 应该在哪里行动，人应该在哪里决策，哪些流程应该委托给模型，哪些流程应该被彻底重建。

在 Vattention，我们正在为 Human-AI Cocreation 构建产品。我们不相信未来只是「一键生成，然后人类鼓掌」。我们真正感兴趣的是：AI 如何理解创作意图，如何持续存在于 workflow 之中，如何预判下一步动作，并与人协作产出更好的作品。

真正的 Taste，是能够判断结构：

什么才是正确的架构？
什么只是看起来 AI-native？
什么能在 demo 里成立，却会在生产环境里失败？
哪里可以快，哪里必须准？

Calibration：持续贴近现实

如果 Taste 是方向感，那么 Calibration 就是对现实的承诺。

在 AI 里，人很容易被表层进展打动。一个 demo 看起来很强，但在真实 workflow 里可能失败；一个模型听起来很聪明，但可能并不可靠；一个团队看起来很忙，但可能正在远离核心问题。

Calibration 是一种持续追问的纪律：

我们的判断在哪里偏离了现实？
执行在哪里偏离了我们的标准？
团队当前状态在哪里偏离了目标？
我们是不是在用旧世界的假设解释新世界的系统？

没有 Calibration，Taste 会变成神秘主义。
有了 Calibration，Taste 才会成为一种工程能力和组织能力。

我们在寻找什么样的人

Taste 是 AI 时代的架构判断力。
Calibration 是持续与现实对齐的纪律。

没有 Calibration 的 Taste，会漂向抽象。
没有 Taste 的 Calibration，也许稳定，但不会带来真正的改变。

我们寻找同时拥有两者的人：能看见新的结构，但愿意被现实修正；能做非共识判断，但会用真实执行检验判断；知道如何领导 AI，也知道如何让团队持续与真实世界对齐。

AI 时代不会奖励那些只是会使用 AI 工具的人。

它会奖励那些能够重新设计系统、重新思考协作、重新定义工作方式的人。

这就是我们所说的 Taste + Calibration。

也是 Bobby 成为 Vattention Partner 所代表的东西。

Recently, we made Bobby Chen a Partner at Vattention.

This was not simply a new title. It was a way of formally recognizing something that had already become true: Bobby was already operating with the responsibility, judgment, and ownership of a partner.

In just a few months, he took on critical problems, helped turn ambiguity into structure, and pushed the team toward a higher standard of execution. In a traditional organization, that timeline might seem unusually fast. In an AI startup, we think it is the right speed.

Partnership as a Dynamic Responsibility

At Vattention, partnership is a dynamic mechanism, not a fixed rank.

It is not a permanent badge that one receives and stores away. Of course, we hope every Partner continues to lead the company over the long term. But structurally, we think of partnership as a way to decouple authority, responsibility, and economics.

Responsibility comes from the mission someone is actually carrying.
Authority comes from their role in shaping direction and making critical decisions at a given stage.
Economics should reflect long-term contribution and real impact.

In this sense, partnership is not granted by tenure. It is recognized through responsibility, contribution, and the ability to drive meaningful change.

Bobby's transition into this role was not an exception to the system. It was the system working as intended.

From Linear Headcount to 100-1000x Leverage

In many organizations, adding people is assumed to increase output.

One more person means more capacity. A strong hire may contribute 1.5x; an average hire may still contribute 1.0x.

In the AI era, this assumption is less reliable.

Every new person adds communication cost, alignment cost, coordination cost, and operational entropy. Whether they increase output is uncertain. Whether they increase system complexity is almost guaranteed.

We are moving from a world of 1.5 vs. 1.0 to a world of 100-1000 vs. -3.

The right person can create 100-1000x leverage. They can redefine the problem, redesign the workflow, place AI in the right part of the system, and shift the capability frontier of the entire team.

The wrong person is not merely a 0.8x contributor. They can be negative leverage: increasing complexity, slowing down decision-making, and making the organization better at explaining itself than confronting reality.

This is why we describe our talent philosophy with two words:

Taste + Calibration.

Taste: Knowing How to Lead AI

By Taste, we do not mean preference or aesthetics in the usual sense.

We mean architectural taste: the judgment required to understand how to lead AI.

Using AI and leading AI are different capabilities.

Using AI means treating it as a faster tool.
Leading AI means redesigning the system around it: where AI should act, where humans should decide, which workflows should be delegated to models, and which workflows should be rebuilt entirely.

At Vattention, we are building for Human-AI Cocreation. We do not believe the future is simply "one-click generation, followed by human applause." We are interested in how AI can understand creative intent, remain present inside a workflow, anticipate the next action, and collaborate with people to produce better work.

Real Taste means being able to judge structure:

What is the right architecture?
What only looks AI-native?
What works in a demo but fails in production?
Where can we move fast, and where must we be precise?

Calibration: Staying Close to Reality

If Taste is a sense of direction, Calibration is a commitment to reality.

In AI, it is easy to be impressed by surface-level progress. A demo may look powerful but fail in a real workflow. A model may sound intelligent but be unreliable. A team may look busy while drifting away from the core problem.

Calibration is the discipline of continuously asking:

Where does our judgment diverge from reality?
Where does execution diverge from our standard?
Where does the team's current state diverge from the goal?
Are we using old-world assumptions to explain a new-world system?

Without Calibration, Taste becomes mysticism.
With Calibration, Taste becomes an engineering and organizational capability.

What We Are Looking For

Taste is architectural judgment for the AI era.
Calibration is the discipline of staying aligned with reality.

Taste without Calibration drifts into abstraction.
Calibration without Taste can become stable, but not transformative.

We look for people who have both: people who can see new structures, but are willing to be corrected by reality; people who can make non-consensus judgments, but test those judgments through real execution; people who know how to lead AI, and how to keep a team aligned with the world as it actually is.

The AI era will not reward people who merely know how to use AI tools.

It will reward people who can redesign systems, rethink collaboration, and redefine how work gets done.

That is what we mean by Taste + Calibration.

And that is what Bobby becoming a Partner at Vattention represents.
